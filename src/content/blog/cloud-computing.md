What is Cloud Computing? It's nothing but sharing computer resources instead of using local software or local storage. It's everywhere now — in business, entertainment, education. Though it was in its infancy in 2010, it was already clear it would be one of the defining technologies of the era.

Cloud computing comes into focus when you think about what IT always needs: a way to increase capacity or add capabilities on the fly without investing in new infrastructure or new software licenses. It's any subscription-based or pay-per-use service that, in real time over the Internet, extends existing capabilities.

Different people have very different perspectives on it.

## Views From the Industry

**Richard Stallman, FSF Founder:**
*"It's stupidity. It's worse than stupidity: it's a marketing hype campaign. One reason you should not use web applications to do your computing is that you lose control."*

**Sergey Brin, Google Co-Founder:**
*"I think whether you're an enterprise or an individual, it rarely makes sense to have your own buildings, fiber cables and infrastructure. Ultimately, your resources are available to you wherever you are, and you want them there. There's an economy of scale that makes that easier and more powerful."*

**Jonathan Schwartz, CEO Sun Microsystems:**
*"Cloud computing is the future of Open Source."*

**Bill Gates, Co-Founder of Microsoft:**
*"In the extreme case, we can take somebody's data center and run it for them on the cloud. All the issues about administrative, capacity, who owns the data — that's cloud computing, and there is a lot of deep invention and work."*

## Where I Stand

Stallman's concern about losing control is legitimate and worth taking seriously. But the economic and practical arguments for cloud computing are also real. The tension between convenience and control is one the technology industry is still working through — and will be for a long time.

What matters is who controls the cloud infrastructure and under what terms. An open web needs open, accountable infrastructure.
