*"Make something amazing with the Web!"*

In the first week of March, I attended the Reps Training Days at Athens, Greece, organized by the Mozilla Foundation. 40 amazing Reps from all over the world came together to meet, discuss, and brainstorm on taking the [Mozilla Webmaker Project](https://www.webmaker.org/) forward. All from different backgrounds, different languages. One thing in common: we love the Web.

## Day 1: Hive Athens Pop-up Event

It was a great learning experience — people from different environments coming together to teach and learn with the latest technologies. I was electrified. I learned some robotics and played with electricity!

## The Training Days

Two full days of brainstorming and discussions on Webmaker tools, events, mentors, kits, hacktivities. Each person brought a different perspective and we made the walls incredibly colorful with ideas.

## The Test Event at British Council

The part I loved most. Teaching kids is fun.

I was amazed to see the kids already knew that Popcorn Maker is a video remixing tool and not an actual popcorn maker!

We had proper hacktivities and an agenda prepared. Everything was immediately hacked — we tried to hack the British Council computer system right from the start.

The kids I trained were 13 years old and they created something amazing with the web. We used Popcorn Maker to remix lots of videos. One kid remixed a video for an F1 competition at his school (he's also a web developer at his school — legend).

Firefox wasn't updated on their laptops so it took a while, but IE was surprisingly generous and Popcorn Maker worked like a charm.

**Suggestions from the kids for Popcorn Maker:**

1. An option to remix two or more videos at a time
2. A download option
3. A 5-minute plain white background video to create new content from scratch

> "Would've been great if we did a full day event rather than a half day event."

That's the kind of feedback that makes all the travel worth it.

Thanks to Michelle Thorne, Laura Hilliger, Mark, and Gunner. You guys are amazing — I still wonder how you stay that energetic all day!

*You know code or you don't — and you want to make something amazing with the web? Mozilla Webmaker is the place.*
