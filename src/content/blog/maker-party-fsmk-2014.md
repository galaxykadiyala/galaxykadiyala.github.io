Every year [FSMK](http://fsmk.org) organizes a 9 day summer camp in Bangalore. Students from different colleges all over Karnataka join to learn, create, and collaborate.

Last year's response for Maker Party at the Summer Camp was overwhelming and the organizers invited us to host Maker Party this year too!

[Maker Party](https://party.webmaker.org/), Mozilla's annual global campaign to teach the web, kicked off on July 15!

## The Sessions

As always Ankit started with what is Open Source, Open Web, and why Mozilla — explaining briefly about all the Webmaker tools: X-ray Goggles, Thimble, Popcorn, Appmaker.

Then it was time to make things! The students were amazed to see how easy it is to view the HTML source of a webpage with X-ray Goggles and replace the code to generate something new. We gave a [set of links](http://bit.ly/fsmklinks) to start hands-on for those who wanted to remix something already available.

*And yes — we had more than 100 Webmaker makes in just 3 hours! Webmaker tools take you from n00b to ninja in less than one hour!*

Webmaker tools are fun and challenging in just the right way — students understood the tools and how they can learn and create something amazing on the Web.

## The Crowd

Most of the students were either Firefox Student Ambassadors or wanted to know more about FSA. They were pretty excited to host their own Maker Parties at their colleges.
