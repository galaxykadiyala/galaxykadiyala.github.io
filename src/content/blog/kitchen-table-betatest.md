I hosted a small kitchen table event called 'Hack it!' and it was just awesome. Simple and yet more productive than you'd expect. We were 5 people including two children under 15.

## How It Went

It was a 45 minute event. I gave a 10-minute overview on using [Hackasaurus X-ray Goggles](http://hackasaurus.org/) to inspect code and remix a webpage — and then the children just started hacking away.

It became a hobby for them. They now remix websites using all their creativity whenever they get the chance.

## Why Kitchen Table Events Work

You don't need a venue, a sponsor, or a big audience. You just need a couple of enthusiastic people, 20 minutes, and a table.

If you want to run your own: [Kitchen Table event guide](https://webmaker.org/en-US/events/guides/kitchen-table/).

Go host one. Seriously.
