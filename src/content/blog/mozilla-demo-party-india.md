It was June 25th morning and I was genuinely nervous. How will it go? Will the audience actively participate? Will they enjoy it?

Voila! It was a great success.

The Mozilla India & Mozilla Labs Demo Party at Pune — the only Demo Party to happen in India since 1999. Not everyone knows about Demo Party culture in India, which made this feel extra special.

Everyone arrived on time (the event started half an hour late due to network issues, but still). I gave an introduction to the Demo Party and Demo Scene culture. Tobias from Mozilla sent an exclusive video message to the party. All smiles when he removes his tee and goes surfing at the end.

## The Talks

**Aditee from Microsoft** gave an overview of what HTML5 is — and what it's not (not "How To Meet Ladies ver 5.0"!). She showed simple demos using HTML5 audio, video, canvas, and transitions — no Flash required.

**Shwetank from Opera Software** showed why designers should care about CSS3. Rounded corners, custom fonts, animations, transitions, drop shadows — all with minimal code, working across devices. CSS3 changes everything.

**Dipen (QED42) and Meher (Amiworks)** talked on HTML5 APIs and new semantics. Meher also explained WebM — a free, open video format designed for use with HTML5 video.

## The Takeaway

In all the talks one thread ran through everything: **cross-domain compatibility**. Whatever you build, it should work on all browsers. Write standard code, add vendor-specific prefixes where needed, make it future-proof.

The participants left enlightened about the latest web technologies and ready to build something interesting. That's a good day.

Thanks to Tobias, Vineel, Abhishek, Raphael Jacob — and to Microsoft, Opera, Amiworks, QED42, Amazing Pune, and SICSR for partnering with Mozilla to make this happen.
