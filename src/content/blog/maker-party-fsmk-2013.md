The Maker Party event at FSMK Summer Camp Bangalore was just amazing!

The [Free Software Movement Karnataka](http://fsmk.org) is one of those communities which promotes open and free source initiatives. They recently conducted a 9 day Summer Camp for Engineering and Science students at JVIT college and asked us if we could include a Mozilla Webmaker event — and gave us one full day for Maker Party!

Gauthamraj and I planned the event organizing. Thanks to Gauthamraj for sorting everything and helping the event actually happen!

## The Sessions

On the event day we had our awesome Reps — Ankit, Jai Pradeesh, Vineel — and a couple of Mozillians, Jafar and Jayakumar, to help with the workshops.

Ankit started by showing how to hack a website. The hands-on workshop went really well and we had so many creative hacks — one of them used Popcorn to call out the volunteers!

The internet was very slow on the event day but offline sources kept things running smoothly.

## The Impact

The event was a huge success. I still see people using the Webmaker tools when I attend meetups — using Popcorn and Thimble for creating invitations and videos.

One participant summed it up perfectly:

*"The next day was probably the only day which I can never forget. The Mozilla Webmaker party was just freaking amazing. It was so simple to modify any website, to give it a touch of our own personalization."*

That's the whole point.
