This is a pilot event and there will be more such trainings in the future!

Last weekend on Feb 14, we invited 50 [Firefox Student Ambassadors](http://fsa.mozilla.org/) from different states in India to join us at the Mozilla Community Space in Bangalore for a fun-filled bootcamp. This is a one day event in Bangalore, our biggest FSA focused event so far in India after revamping the program. We received 80 applicants and selected 50 of them to attend the training.

**Firefox Student Ambassadors is one of the biggest and active ambassador programs in India. As I write, we have 28790 registered students globally with 16700 students from India.**

It was a packed agenda for the day filled with awesomeness, live video talks, ice breakers and hands-on sessions.

## Firefox Hello Demo

Arcadio Lainez, Product Marketing Manager gave us a live demo of [Firefox Hello](https://www.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/hello/) explaining all the features and future plans. It's a feature about communication and staying in touch with people while you browse the Internet — built into the browser using WebRTC (Real-Time Technology).

## Program Insights

TJ, Community Manager for FSA spoke about the program insights — how the ambassador program is designed to provide career development and a way to gain new skills with the 'Approach, Engage, Equip and Empower' idea, and not just a marketing program. 2015 will be the year for creating more resources, increasing the community with active participation from Firefox Clubs in universities.

## Icebreaker!

The ambassadors were divided into 4 groups and asked to discuss:

**Things they like about Mozilla:**
- Working with like minded people
- Firefox OS
- FSA Program being one of the largest student ambassador communities
- Mozilla products and privacy
- Mozilla encourages people to contribute and gain knowledge about the open web
- Maker Party

**One thing they want to learn:**
- Developing apps for Firefox OS
- Tips to grow regional communities
- How to conduct events
- Talk, communicate and learn from others

**What attracted them to Mozilla:**
- A Mozilla booth organized by a Mozilla Rep
- Local community events
- Getting a beginner badge
- Friends who are writing code for Firefox

## Wrapping Up

The FSA Exec Board members explained the program structure, recognition, contribution ways to Mozilla, club activities, club lead training, IRC — and the day ended with a Firefox OS hackathon.

Thanks to our RALs (Santosh Viswanatham, Bala Subramaniyan) and Vineel Reddy for joining and helping with the event. It's safe to say everyone had a good time, and it was amazing to have a group of ambassadors all in one place!

The participants gave feedback that the bootcamp helped motivate them and equipped them with resources to share their knowledge with others. We look forward to seeing the ambassadors putting all this into action at their universities.
