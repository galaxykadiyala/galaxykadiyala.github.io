The Mozilla India Community Meetup '14 far surpassed my expectations. This is the first time a large number of contributors joined the community meet-up in India and it was collaborative, fun, and productive all at once.

The Mozilla India Community Meetup is an invitation-only gathering of some of the most active contributors in the Mozilla India community. There were brainstorming sessions, SWOT analysis, informative breakout sessions, ten-minute QUILT updates from Task Force Teams, an Australis update, and user research of Firefox OS in India.

I'm lucky to be part of the organizing team. There were a total of 65 community members: staff, hackers, localizers, events, marketing, testers, support, WoMoz, FSAs, and users.

## Day 1: Task Force Teams Meet-up

I traveled all night from Bangalore to Hyderabad (with severe flu — took heavy medication to survive the weekend) and arrived Friday morning just in time for the Task Force meeting. I spent most of the day working on events SOP/process with my partner in crime, Vineel.

We did brain-swarming to list all the challenges and how to proceed further as the community grows. All task force teams (L10n, Technical, Support, Webmaker, ReMo, Engagement) gave a 10-minute update on accomplishments from Jan 2013 to April 2014.

The rest of the attendees arrived in the evening and we all went out for a long dinner — a chance to get to know each other properly.

## Day 2: Community Meet-up

The day started with the "I am a Mozillian" video and was filled with quilt updates from all teams:

- What are you most proud of in 2013?
- What was your biggest challenge in 2013?
- What is your big goal for 2014?

Gen gave a talk on the Australis launch that was going out with Firefox 29 on April 29. The day wrapped with discussion on the ReMo program with the whole community. Quality over quantity was a key theme — mentors limited to ~10 mentees for better relationships.

## Day 3: Wrap-up

More laid back. I joined with Webmaker, FSA, Design and Technical teams discussing how to get users engaged — online events, offline events, mozillaindia.org, social media.

Then Jane Hsu joined us online from Taiwan for a Firefox OS market research session with the audience. The Firefox OS rickshaw covers campaign idea was brilliant (in India, rickshaw/tuk-tuk is the best way to travel in the city).

Technical Task Force managed to rip the Reps events page into mozillaindia.org in just a few hours.

## Day 4: Departure

A few of us stayed for a Hyderabad tour — Charminar, the Chowmahalla Palace, and some excellent Hyderabad Biryani with Brian, Madalina, Vineel and Deb.

The Community India Meet-up was truly an amazing experience. I met so many people from the community for the first time. Happy, excited, and invigorated. Now back to Bangalore. Lel.
