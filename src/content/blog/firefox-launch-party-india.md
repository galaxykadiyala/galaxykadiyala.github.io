Launch parties are special. There's something about gathering people around a shared moment — a software release — that brings out the best in the Mozilla community.

## The Energy

Walking into a room full of people genuinely excited about a browser release is something most people in tech don't get to experience. But Mozilla events have always been different. People show up because they care.

## What We Did

Demos, discussions, and a lot of enthusiasm. We showed off new features, talked about why an independent browser matters, and recruited a few new contributors along the way.

## The Ripple Effect

Every event like this plants a seed. Some attendees go on to become contributors. Some just leave with a better understanding of why the open web matters. Both outcomes are wins.
