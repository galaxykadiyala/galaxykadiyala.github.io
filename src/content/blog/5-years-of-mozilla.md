Five years ago I started contributing to Mozilla almost by accident. I attended a Firefox launch party in Bangalore, got swept up in the energy, and never really left.

## What Mozilla Means

Mozilla isn't just a browser company. It's a community of people who believe the internet should be open, accessible, and owned by everyone — not locked behind proprietary walls. That mission matters more now than ever.

## The Community

The people make it. From the folks I met at my first install fest to the mentors who helped me become a Mozilla Rep — every interaction has shaped how I think about technology and community.

## Five More Years

If the first five taught me anything, it's that open source communities are fragile and powerful at the same time. They need care, new contributors, and people willing to show up consistently.

Here's to five more.
