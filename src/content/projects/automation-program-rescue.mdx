## The problem

A platform-wide test automation program at an enterprise data security and cloud management client was on its third TPM in two months. Seven product teams across India, Europe, and the US, mixed UI and backend stacks, no prior manual QA baseline, no dependency map.

## What I did

Built the proactive risk plan during the planning phase — onboarding docs and videos for fast ramp on attrition. Established cross-timezone handoff artifacts. Mid-program, identified the dependency map gap that was causing cascading failures and rebuilt the sequencing. Made the call to PIP and replace underperforming engineers without losing program velocity, because the onboarding kit was already in place. Hired a DevOps engineer to rebuild the broken CI/CD pipelines.

## The outcome

Delivered in 10 months instead of 8, but stabilised after two TPMs had failed. The client publicly thanked the team and named my work specifically. The program shipped, and the dependency map mistake remains the most instructive thing I've learned about coordinating effort across teams.
